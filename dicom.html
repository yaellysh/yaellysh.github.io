DICOM Image Conversion

Project Summary

I developed a comprehensive Python pipeline designed to convert sequences of 2D Cartesian PNG images into 3D DICOM files, significantly enhancing their utility in medical imaging applications. This project involved implementing advanced algorithms for precise spatial alignment and volumetric reconstruction, ensuring that the resulting 3D models were both accurate and of high quality. To validate and visualize the generated 3D DICOM files, I utilized industry-standard DICOM viewers such as Weasis, 3D Slicer, and OsiriX, confirming the effectiveness and reliability of the solution.

The goal of this project was to create a proof of concept to allow for future developments once sponsorship was achieved. Theoretically this project could be used to create to-scale 3D models of health data, allowing health professionals to get accurate measurements and use these specifications to create new implants.
